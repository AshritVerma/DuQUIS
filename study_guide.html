Study Guide

Articles

Why now is the right time to study quantum computing? Dec. 2014, by Aram Harrow, Associate Professor of Physics at MIT.

Textbooks

Quantum Computation and Quantum Information, by Michael Nielsen & Isaac Chuang, Cambridge University Press, 2010. An online version is available at Duke Library. Some of the solutions can be found here [github:goropikari] or here.

Online Lectures and Recordings

  • Course website for Duke's Quantum Information Science (ECE 523/ PHY 627, Spring 2017) by Professor Jungsang Kim. Complete lecture notes and homework (no solutions) are contained.
  • edX course Quantum Information Science I, Part 1 (MITx: 8.370.1x), Part 2 (MITx: 8.370.2x), and Part 3 (MITx: 8.370.3x), by Professor Peter Shor and Isaac Chuang from MIT. Complete recordings of all lectures, free of charge. Access to assignments requires purchase.
  • Course website for MIT's Quantum Computation (2.111) by Prosessor Issac Chuang. The webpage includes complete problem sets with solutions.

Recommendations from experts

  • Mini-Library on Quantum Information and Computation. By Xiaodi Wu, Assistant Professor of Computer Science at the University of Maryland.
  • Entry Points for Learning Quantum Computing. By Chris Ferrie.
